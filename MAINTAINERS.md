# Maintainers

This document lists who currently maintains the XSI-AIMS specification and how maintainership is expected to broaden. For the governance model these roles sit within, see [`GOVERNANCE.md`](GOVERNANCE.md) and the authoritative [`governance/STEWARDSHIP.md`](governance/STEWARDSHIP.md).

## Current maintainership

Maintainership is currently held collectively by the **XSI-AIMS Working Group**, a team within the initial steward organization. There are no individually named maintainer seats during the pre-2.0 phase; review, merge, and release responsibilities are exercised by the Working Group as a team.

| Team | Organization | Contact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| XSI-AIMS Working Group | Extended Systems Intelligence Corporation (XSI) | aims@xtendedsystems.com |

The Working Group is the team referenced by the repository's `CODEOWNERS` (`@Extended-Systems-Intelligence/aims-maintainers`) and is auto-requested for review on pull requests.

## Responsibilities

The XSI-AIMS Working Group is responsible for:

- triaging Issues and Discussions;
- reviewing and merging pull requests;
- running the public RFC process described in [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md) and recording decisions with their rationale;
- tagging and signing releases under the versioning and freeze conventions in [`governance/STEWARDSHIP.md`](governance/STEWARDSHIP.md);
- upholding the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) and handling [security reports](SECURITY.md).

## Becoming a maintainer

Maintainership is intended to open to external maintainers as the community forms — this is a core part of the stewardship transition, not an afterthought. The expected pathway:

1. **Contribute consistently.** Sustained, high-quality engagement — well-scoped Issues, substantive Discussion participation, RFCs, reviews, accepted contributions — is the foundation.
2. **Become a recognized reviewer.** Contributors with demonstrated subject-matter depth in an area of the specification take on review weight there.
3. **Be invited to maintain.** As reviewers demonstrate sustained, trusted engagement, the Working Group invites them to join as maintainers. Under the stewardship trajectory, the first external maintainer additions happen organically during the v1.x cycle, and a documented multi-organization maintainer structure is established through a Stewardship RFC as the community matures.

Participants from outside XSI are explicitly welcomed into this pathway. The intent is for maintainership — and ultimately release authority — to broaden beyond the initial steward over time, per [`governance/STEWARDSHIP.md`](governance/STEWARDSHIP.md).
